## Why Personal Brand Matters

The best jobs aren't posted on job boards. They come through referrals, speaking invitations, and inbound opportunities. A strong personal brand attracts them to you.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Write Publicly

```markdown
# What to write about:
- Problems you solved this week
- A bug that took 3 hours to fix
- Your setup/config (people love these)
- A library you tried and your honest review
- Tutorial for something you just learned
```

**Where to publish:**
- Personal blog (your domain)
- Dev.to (massive developer audience)
- Hashnode (SEO-optimized, custom domain)
- Medium (general audience)

**Consistency beats quality:** One post per week for a year beats one perfect post per month. Your 50th post will be 10x better than your 1st.

### 2. Contribute to Open Source

```bash
# Start with documentation — always needed, always appreciated
# 1. Find a repo you use
# 2. Look for "good first issue" labels
# 3. Fix a typo in README
# 4. Add a missing example
# 5. Translate docs
# 6. Write a test for an uncovered edge case

# Tools to find issues
gh issue list --label "good first issue" --repo facebook/react
```

**The ladder:**
1. Fix typos → 2. Improve docs → 3. Fix small bugs → 4. Add features → 5. Become maintainer

### 3. Share Knowledge Publicly

| Platform | Best For | Frequency |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Twitter/X | Hot takes, threads, quick tips | Daily |
| LinkedIn | Career insights, long-form | 2-3x/week |
| YouTube | Tutorials, project walkthroughs | Weekly |
| GitHub | Code, tooling, templates | Continuous |
| Conference talks | Deep dives, case studies | 2-3x/year |

## Content Ideas That Always Work

```markdown
1. "How I built X in Y hours" — project walkthroughs
2. "My development setup for 2025" — desk/editor/config tours
3. "X mistakes I made as a junior developer"
4. "Why I switched from X to Y" — technology comparisons
5. "X library you should know about" — tool recommendations
6. "How to solve X problem" — tutorials
7. "X things I wish I knew when starting with Y"
8. "My salary progression from junior to senior"
9. "A day in the life of a [your role]"
10. "X books every developer should read"
```

## Building an Audience

### Twitter/X Strategy

```markdown
- Post 1-2 technical tips daily
- Engage with 5 posts from developers you admire
- Quote-tweet with your perspective (not just retweet)
- Thread your longer content (numbered tweets)
- Use code screenshots (images perform 3x better)
```

### LinkedIn Strategy

```markdown
- Post once every 2-3 days
- Focus on lessons learned and career insights
- Write in short paragraphs (mobile-optimized)
- End with a question to drive comments
- Respond to every comment within 24 hours
```

## Your Personal Website

```html
<!-- The minimum viable developer site -->
<site>
  <hero>Your name, role, one-liner</hero>
  <projects>3-5 best projects with live links</projects>
  <blog>At least 5 posts (show consistency)</blog>
  <contact>Email + social links</contact>
</site>
```

**Must-haves:**
- Custom domain (firstname@domain.com is infinitely more professional than gmail)
- Mobile-first design
- Projects with live demos (not just screenshots)
- Blog with technical content
- Clear way to contact you

## Networking Without Being Sleazy

```markdown
# Good outreach message:
"Hey [name], I used your [library/project] in my recent project
and it saved me hours. I wrote about my experience here:
[link]. Thanks for building it!"

# Bad outreach message:
"Hey, can you give me a job at your company?"
```

**Conferences on a budget:**
- Apply for diversity/inclusion tickets
- Volunteer (free admission + networking)
- Submit a talk (speakers usually get free tickets)
- Watch livestreams and engage in Discord/Slack

## Measuring Success

```markdown
Month 1:  50 blog visitors, 100 Twitter followers
Month 3:  500 blog visitors, 500 Twitter followers
Month 6:  2,000 blog visitors, 2,000 Twitter followers
Month 12: 10,000 blog visitors, 5,000 Twitter followers

Results at each stage:
Month 1-3:  1-2 recruiter messages/month
Month 3-6:  1-2 freelance inquiries/month
Month 6-12: Conference speaking invitations
Month 12+:  Job offers come to you
```

## What NOT to Do

- Don't copy others' content (you'll be found out)
- Don't be overly promotional (80% value, 20% promotion)
- Don't compare your chapter 1 to someone's chapter 20
- Don't wait until you're an "expert" — document your learning journey
- Don't ghost after building a connection

## Conclusion

Your GitHub profile is your resume. Your blog is your interview. Your Twitter is your networking event. Start small, be consistent, and give value before asking for anything in return. Six months from now, you'll wish you started today.
